Plenary speakers

Brooke Feigon

Brooke Feigon (CUNY)

Brooke Feigon is a Professor at CUNY City College of New York. Her work (e.g. on Ramanujan graphs) fuses number theory, automorphic forms, representation theory, combinatorics, and algebraic geometry.

Drew Sutherland

Drew Sutherland (MIT)

Drew Sutherland is a Senior Research Scientist at MIT focusing on both theoretical and computational aspects of number theory and arithmetic geometry.

Wei Zhang

Wei Zhang (MIT)

Wei Zhang is a Professor at MIT. His work includes developing the theory of the relative trace formula to connect L-functions with objects in algebraic geometry.

A bit of history

In 1998, number theorists at Université Laval and the University of Maine founded the "Maine/Québec Conference on Number Theory and Related Topics." Since then it has taken place annually on a weekend in early Fall (except 2001, when it was cancelled due to the terrorist attacks that September). We invite number theorists and mathematicians in adjacent fields from New England, eastern Canada, and beyond to speak about their research and discuss ideas for future work. Each year there is a change in venue: odd years at UMaine, even years at U. Laval.

Practical matters

  • Lodging: Hotel Ursa on UMaine's campus in Orono.
  • Transportation: If traveling to Orono by coach, there is a UMaine stop on the Concord Line. Travel to and from Bangor Airport can be done with Uber or by Taxi.
  • Carpooling to/from the airport: One conference participant has created a Google sheet of arrival/departure times for anyone wishing to carpool or share a taxi.
  • Banquet: The banquet will take place at High Tide restaurant in Brewer. We are chartering a bus from the hotel to the banquet and back, but it isn't big enough for everyone, so we ask that people try to carpool if possible. Participants may be asked to contribute for attending the banquet.
  • Conference: The conference will be held at the UMaine campus in Orono, in D. P. Corbett Hall. Please see the schedule for details.
